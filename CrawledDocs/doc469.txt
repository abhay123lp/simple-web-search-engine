Students on the programme may be immersed in the rich diversity of India or Israel and experience the vibrancy of their entrepreneurial culture. It is also a unique programme that is open to both undergraduate and postgraduate students. Academic Details Participants will not be taking any course but will have to attend a series of workshops on India or Israel and about entrepreneurship at the start of the programme. Students may count the internship to their NUS degree requirement as approved by their respective faculty. Undergraduate students from the Engineering Faculty can apply to their departments to have their three month-long (India) internships recognized as a self-sourced vacation internship programme that is worth 6 modular credits through Self-Initiated VIP Undergraduate students from the Engineering Faculty can apply to their departments have their six month-long (India, Israel) internships recognized as self-sourced industrial attachment that is worth 12 modular credits through Self-Initiated IA Financial Details - NOC Entrepreneurial India Students going on the NOC India programme will be supported with an allowance from NUS but should expect to incur additional out-of-pocket expenses. With these, students are expected to take care of their accommodation, living and transport expenses. NOC covers the following expenses Visa application Round trip airfare Workshop fees Insurance Allowance (as mentioned above) Financial Details - NOC Israel Students selected for the NOC Israel programme will receive an internship allowance/scholarship over the period of 6 months in Israel which will be used to pay for their living cost overseas (accommodation, transportations, meals). Students are also expected to incur a personal monthly expenditure of USD500 a month (approx) to supplement their living expenses according to individual lifestyle. NOC covers the following expenses Visa application Round trip airfare Workshop lectures and materials Insurance Eligibility Students should be enrolled in full-time NUS undergraduate or graduate courses. First year undergraduate students are welcome to apply for this programme. Although you will not be scheduled for interviews, you will be part of the "Friends of NOC" programme where you can learn more about the programme and get better prepared for the selection process. Undergraduate students should have obtained about 60 modular credits for NOC Entrepreneurial India and 80 modular credits for NOC Israel at the point of departure and MUST have at least one remaining semester of study on upon return to NUS without exceeding their maximum candidature. Graduate students should have completed at least one semester of studies at NUS at the point of departure. Students of Indian nationality are not eligible to apply for NOC Entrepreneurial India.   © Copyright 2001- National University of Singapore. All Rights Reserved.
